What Is a Portfolio Overlap Checker?

A portfolio overlap checker is a free online tool that compares two lists of stock or fund names and identifies how many securities are common to both. For Indian investors who hold multiple mutual funds or build custom stock portfolios, overlap analysis is essential because high duplication means you are not as diversified as you think.

Many investors in India unknowingly hold the same large-cap stocks across several mutual funds. For example, Nifty 50 index funds, large-cap funds, and flexi-cap funds often contain similar names like Reliance Industries, TCS, HDFC Bank, and Infosys. Our portfolio overlap checker reveals this hidden concentration so you can rebalance and reduce risk.

How Does the Portfolio Overlap Checker Work?

Using our portfolio overlap checker is simple and takes only a few seconds:

  • Paste Portfolio 1 — Enter a plain-text list of stock or fund names from your first holding.
  • Paste Portfolio 2 — Enter a plain-text list from your second holding.
  • Click Check Overlap — The tool normalises names, finds common entries, and calculates the overlap percentage.

The result shows the number of unique names in each portfolio, the overlap percentage, and a list of common securities. You can paste holdings from mutual fund factsheets, broker statements, or watchlists. This mutual fund overlap insight helps you decide whether to continue holding both funds or switch one for better diversification.

Portfolio Overlap Formula Explained

The portfolio overlap checker uses a straightforward set-based formula:

Overlap % = Common unique names / Total unique names × 100

Where:

  • Common unique names — securities that appear in both portfolios after normalisation.
  • Total unique names — the combined set of distinct securities across both portfolios.

For example, if Portfolio A has 20 unique stocks, Portfolio B has 25 unique stocks, and 10 stocks are common, the total unique names are 35 (20 + 25 − 10). The overlap percentage is 10 ÷ 35 × 100 = 28.57%. This mutual fund overlap score tells you that roughly 29% of your combined holdings are duplicated.

Real-Life Example: Mutual Fund Overlap in India

Consider Ravi, an investor in Mumbai who holds two popular equity funds. He copies the top holdings from each fund's monthly factsheet and pastes them into our portfolio overlap checker:

  • Fund A holdings: Reliance Industries, TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, ICICI Bank, Hindustan Unilever, Bajaj Finance, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Axis Bank, L&T
  • Fund B holdings: TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, ICICI Bank, Bharti Airtel, Maruti Suzuki, Sun Pharma, Axis Bank, ITC, SBI

The portfolio overlap checker finds 6 common names: TCS, HDFC Bank, Infosys, ICICI Bank, Axis Bank. Total unique names are 15. The overlap percentage is 6 ÷ 15 × 100 = 40%. Ravi realises that 40% of his combined equity exposure is concentrated in the same stocks. He decides to replace Fund B with a mid-cap or international fund to improve diversification.

Benefits of Using a Portfolio Overlap Checker

A dedicated portfolio overlap checker offers several advantages for Indian investors:

  • Uncover hidden concentration — Discover when multiple funds hold the same stocks, reducing effective diversification.
  • Reduce redundancy — Eliminate overlapping holdings and free up capital for different sectors or asset classes.
  • Improve risk management — Lower exposure to single-stock risk by spreading across truly different securities.
  • Quick screening — Compare any two portfolios in seconds without downloading software or creating accounts.
  • Fund comparison — Evaluate mutual fund overlap before adding a new fund to your existing portfolio.
  • Privacy first — Your portfolio lists are processed for the request only and are never stored.
