What Is a SIP Calculator?

A SIP calculator India is a free online tool that helps investors estimate the future value of their Systematic Investment Plan. SIPs allow you to invest a fixed amount every month into mutual funds, building wealth gradually through disciplined investing and the power of compounding. Our SIP calculator India tool takes your monthly contribution, expected annual return, and investment duration to project your maturity amount, total invested capital, and estimated returns.

Whether you are planning for retirement, your child's education, a home down payment, or simply building an emergency corpus, using a reliable SIP calculator monthly investment planner gives you clarity before committing real money. Unlike complex spreadsheets, this calculator delivers instant results without storing any personal data.

How Does the SIP Calculator Work?

Our SIP calculator India uses the standard future-value formula for recurring monthly investments. You only need to enter three inputs:

  • Monthly investment — the fixed amount you plan to invest every month (minimum ₹100).
  • Expected annual return rate — your assumed yearly growth percentage based on the mutual fund category and market outlook.
  • Investment duration — the number of years you intend to stay invested.

Once you click "Calculate SIP," the tool instantly computes your maturity value, total amount invested, and the wealth gained through compounding. You can adjust inputs repeatedly to compare different sip calculator monthly investment scenarios and find a plan that fits your budget and goals.

SIP Formula Explained

The mathematics behind a SIP calculator India is straightforward. The formula used is:

FV = P × [((1 + r)n − 1) / r] × (1 + r)

Where:

  • FV = Future value or maturity amount
  • P = Monthly investment amount
  • r = Monthly rate of return (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • n = Total number of months (years × 12)

The extra (1 + r) factor at the end assumes the SIP is invested at the beginning of each month, which is the standard practice for most mutual fund SIPs in India. This small detail can make a meaningful difference over long durations.
