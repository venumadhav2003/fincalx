What Is an Income Tax Calculator?

An income tax calculator India is a free online tool that helps resident individuals estimate their tax liability for a given financial year. Instead of manually applying slab rates, cess, and deductions, you simply enter your gross income, choose your tax regime, and the calculator delivers an instant estimate of taxable income, base tax, health and education cess, and total tax payable.

For FY 2025-26, Indian taxpayers can choose between the old regime with exemptions and deductions, or the new regime with lower slab rates but fewer deductions. Our income tax calculator India supports both options, making it easy to perform a tax calculator old vs new regime comparison and pick the structure that saves you more money.

How Does the Income Tax Calculator Work?

Using our income tax calculator India is straightforward. You need to provide:

  • Annual gross income — your total yearly earnings before any deductions or exemptions.
  • Tax regime — select "New regime" for lower rates with limited deductions, or "Old regime" if you claim deductions under Sections 80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, and others.
  • Old regime deductions — enter the total deductions you expect to claim. This field is used only when the old regime is selected.

Once you submit, the calculator applies the current FY 2025-26 slab structure, subtracts eligible deductions, computes base tax, adds 4% health and education cess, and presents a clear breakdown. You can switch regimes and recalculate instantly to complete your tax calculator old vs new regime analysis.

Tax Calculator Old vs New Regime: Which Is Better?

The most common question among Indian taxpayers is whether to stick with the old regime or move to the new one. The answer depends on your income level and the deductions you actually claim.

Under the new regime, slab rates are lower, but you cannot claim most deductions like Section 80C (₹1.5 lakh), 80D (health insurance), HRA exemption, or home loan interest under Section 24. Under the old regime, you pay higher slab rates but can reduce taxable income significantly if you have substantial deductions.

A tax calculator old vs new regime comparison is the fastest way to settle this. Generally, taxpayers with high deductions (home loans, insurance, PF, NPS) benefit from the old regime, while those with minimal deductions may pay less under the new regime. Our calculator lets you test both scenarios in seconds.

Real-Life Example: Salaried Employee in Pune

Meet Ananya, a software engineer in Pune with an annual gross income of ₹12,00,000. She pays ₹1,50,000 under Section 80C (PPF + ELSS), ₹25,000 under Section 80D for health insurance, and claims HRA exemption of ₹1,20,000. Using our income tax calculator India:

  • Old regime: Taxable income drops to ₹9,05,000 after deductions. Base tax is approximately ₹1,01,000 plus cess, totalling around ₹1,05,040.
  • New regime: Taxable income is ₹11,75,000 after standard deduction. Base tax is approximately ₹1,42,500 plus cess, totalling around ₹1,48,200.

In Ananya's case, the old regime saves her over ₹43,000. However, if she had no major deductions, the new regime could be cheaper. This is why running a tax calculator old vs new regime check every year is essential, especially before tax-saving investment deadlines.
